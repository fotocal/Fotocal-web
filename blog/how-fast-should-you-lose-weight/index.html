How Fast Should You Lose Weight?

The internet promises rapid results. Your body prefers a pace that keeps your muscle, your energy and your sanity intact.

May 25, 2026·5 min read

When people decide to lose weight, they usually want it gone yesterday. But the rate you choose shapes the outcome — not just how fast you get there, but how much muscle you keep and whether it stays off.

The sweet spot

Most people do best losing about 0.5–1% of bodyweight per week. For someone at 80kg, that’s roughly 0.4–0.8kg weekly. Fast enough to stay motivated, slow enough to protect muscle.

Why faster backfires

  • Muscle loss: aggressive deficits burn muscle alongside fat, leaving you smaller but soft.
  • Rebound hunger: extreme restriction spikes appetite and triggers binges.
  • Energy and mood crashes: too little food tanks workouts and willpower.
  • Nutrient gaps: very low calories make it hard to get enough nutrients.

When faster can make sense

People with more weight to lose can safely lose a bit faster at first, since a larger body supports a larger deficit. As you lean out, slow down to preserve muscle and adherence.

The real goal: keep it off

Studies consistently show that how you lose weight predicts whether you keep it off. Crash approaches rebound; gradual, habit-based approaches stick. Patience is a feature, not a compromise.

Key takeaways

  • Aim for 0.5–1% of bodyweight lost per week.
  • Rapid loss costs muscle and triggers rebound.
  • Heavier starters can lose faster early, then slow down.
  • How you lose it decides whether it stays off.
