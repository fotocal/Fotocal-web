NEAT: The Hidden Calorie Burn You’re Ignoring

The calories you burn fidgeting, walking and pottering around can outweigh your gym sessions — and you barely notice them.

March 14, 2026·5 min read

When people think about burning calories, they picture workouts. But for most of us, formal exercise is a small slice of daily energy expenditure. A much bigger, more flexible slice is NEAT — Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis — the calories you burn through all the movement that isn’t deliberate exercise.

What counts as NEAT

Walking, taking the stairs, carrying groceries, cleaning, cooking, fidgeting, standing, playing with kids — all of it. Individually tiny, collectively huge. NEAT can vary by hundreds of calories a day between two people of the same size.

Why it matters for weight

NEAT is one reason some people seem to “eat anything” and stay lean — they unconsciously move more. It’s also why dieting can stall: as you eat less, your body often quietly reduces NEAT (you fidget less, walk slower) to conserve energy, shrinking your deficit without you noticing.

How to boost NEAT

  • Set a step goal: a daily target (say 8,000–10,000 steps) is the simplest lever.
  • Walk after meals: even 10 minutes helps movement and blood sugar.
  • Take the stairs and park further away.
  • Stand and move during calls and breaks.

Key takeaways

  • NEAT is everyday non-exercise movement — often bigger than workouts.
  • It varies hugely between people and can quietly drop while dieting.
  • A daily step goal is the easiest way to raise it.
  • NEAT burns calories sustainably, without heavy recovery cost.
