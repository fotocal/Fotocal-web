Portion Control: Simple Tricks That Work

You don’t always need to eat different foods — often you just need to eat a sensible amount of the ones you already love.

April 19, 2026·5 min read

Portion sizes have grown dramatically over the decades. Plates are bigger, packaging is bigger, and restaurant servings are enormous. Much overeating isn’t about willpower — it’s about environments that quietly encourage larger amounts. These tricks push back.

Use smaller plates and glasses

The same portion looks generous on a small plate and skimpy on a large one, and we serve ourselves — and eat — accordingly. Simply downsizing your dinnerware nudges portions down without any sense of deprivation.

Serve, then put it away

Plate your food in the kitchen and store the rest before sitting down. Eating family-style with serving dishes on the table makes second helpings almost automatic.

Learn hand portions

Your hand is a portable guide: a palm of protein, a cupped hand of carbs, a thumb of fats, a fist of vegetables. It’s not exact, but it’s consistent and always available.

Slow down

It takes about 20 minutes for fullness signals to reach your brain. Eating slowly — putting the fork down between bites — lets those signals catch up, so you stop at satisfied instead of stuffed.

Beware package portions

Eating straight from a bag or tub has no natural stopping point. Portion snacks into a bowl instead, and check how many servings a “single” package really contains.

Key takeaways

  • Bigger plates and packages drive bigger portions.
  • Use smaller dishes and serve away from the table.
  • Hand portions give a consistent, portable guide.
  • Slow down and never eat straight from the package.
